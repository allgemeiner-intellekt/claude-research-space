# Research Log — Patriarchal Ideology: Steelmanning the Strongest Arguments

## Scoping — 2026-04-02

**User context**: Finds patriarchal ideology hard to understand, suspects it has
no real theoretical depth, emotionally disagrees with it. Wants a critical study
narrow enough to gain preliminary understanding.

**Scoping rationale**: The most useful thing is NOT a broad survey of patriarchy
as a sociological concept (the user would find that boring and already agrees with
feminist critiques). Instead: find the *strongest* intellectual articulations of
patriarchal ideology — the steelman — and critically evaluate them. This serves
the user's actual need: understanding *why* smart people have believed this, and
whether their arguments have substance or are sophisticated motivated reasoning.

### Narrowed Questions

1. **What are the strongest philosophical arguments for patriarchal social order,
   and where do they break?** — Trace the lineage from Aristotle's "natural
   hierarchy" through Filmer's divine-right patriarchalism, through contemporary
   conservative arguments (e.g., natural law theory, certain readings of
   Confucian role ethics). Identify which arguments have real logical structure
   vs. which smuggle in their conclusions.

2. **Does evolutionary psychology provide a credible scientific basis for
   patriarchal norms, or does it commit the naturalistic fallacy at scale?** —
   The evo-psych argument (sex differences in behavior → gendered social roles
   are "natural") is probably the most common sophisticated-seeming justification
   today. Evaluate the actual evidence quality and the is/ought gap.

3. **How do the most intellectually serious feminist critics dismantle these
   arguments?** — Not general feminist theory, but the specific counter-arguments
   to the steelmanned positions above. Where are the knockouts vs. genuine
   ongoing tensions?

These three questions form a dialectic: thesis (strongest pro-patriarchy
arguments), antithesis (strongest rebuttals), and critical evaluation of where
the debate actually stands. This should give the user a compact but rigorous
understanding of the intellectual terrain.
