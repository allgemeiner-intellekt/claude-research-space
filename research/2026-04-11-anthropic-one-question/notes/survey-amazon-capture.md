# Survey: Is Anthropic in Substance a Captured Amazon Subsidiary?

## Summary

The Amazon–Anthropic relationship is structurally deep but formally constrained. Amazon has invested $8 billion across three tranches (2023–2024), holds a minority equity stake with no board seat, and has been publicly confirmed to hold no board observer rights or formal decision-making power. However, the FTC's January 2025 Section 6(b) staff report — which reviewed actual contract terms under subpoena — found that CSP partners in these AI deals hold "certain consultation, control, and exclusivity rights" to varying degrees, and that AI developers are contractually required to spend large portions of the investment back on their cloud partner's services. The non-cash structure (Project Rainier on Trainium2, deep silicon co-design, AWS as "primary training partner") creates functional lock-in that the formal governance picture understates. The most significant development since mid-2024 is that Anthropic has been actively diversifying: a massive Google TPU deal (Oct 2025, 1 GW+), a $21B Broadcom/TPU deal (April 2026, 3.5 GW), and Microsoft/Nvidia participation in its Feb 2026 $30B raise. This multi-cloud strategy weakens the "captured subsidiary" thesis even as the computational entanglement with AWS remains real. The sharpest falsifiable question is whether the cloud-spend commitments embedded in Amazon's investment agreement constitute de facto exclusivity for model training — something the FTC report flagged but whose specific terms remain redacted.

### FTC Staff Report on AI Partnerships & Investments (January 17, 2025)
- **Link**: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2025/01/ftc-issues-staff-report-ai-partnerships-investments-study

- **Assessment**: The gold standard source for this question. This is a federal agency report based on compelled document production, not press releases. The redacted terms are maddening — the exact "consultation, control, and exclusivity rights" Amazon holds are not disclosed. This is precisely where the answer to the falsifiable question lives.

## Landscape Assessment

**State of knowledge**

The factual chronology is reasonably well-documented through public sources: Amazon invested $1.25B (Sept 2023), then exercised an option for $2.75B (March 2024), then invested an additional $4B (November 2024), for a cumulative $8B. Valuations at each tranche are less clear — the September 2023 deal was reportedly at a $4–5B pre-money valuation; March 2024 at roughly $18B implied; November 2024 at a $40B implied (per The Information, as cited in TechCrunch). By September 2025 the valuation was $183B and by February 2026, $380B. Amazon's $8B stake represents progressively smaller equity as the company has grown. The $8B at a $380B post-money valuation is roughly 2.1% of the company — a tiny ownership stake for what is nominally an $8B investment, suggesting heavy use of preferred equity structures with liquidation preferences rather than common stock.

The non-cash structure is real and consequential. Project Rainier is a massive Trainium2 cluster built specifically for Anthropic. Anthropic engineers are writing low-level silicon kernels for AWS chips — this is not a typical cloud-customer relationship, it is deep co-development. The FTC found CSPs hold "consultation, control, and exclusivity rights" in AI developer partners and that AI developers must spend large portions of investment back on cloud services. The specific terms for the Amazon–Anthropic deal remain redacted.

The governance picture formally favors independence: no Amazon board seat, no board observer rights (confirmed multiple times by both parties during the CMA inquiry, a legally significant context). The LTBT structure provides an additional institutional barrier to investor capture, though the LTBT's own governance (who selects LTBT members?) is not fully documented in publicly available sources.

**Agreements across sources**
- Amazon holds no board seat and no board observer rights: confirmed by Amazon, Anthropic, and implied by CMA's failure to find "material influence" meeting its legal threshold.
- AWS is Anthropic's primary cloud and training partner: confirmed by both parties' official communications.
- Project Rainier is a massive, multi-data-center Trainium2 cluster for Anthropic: confirmed by SiliconANGLE's detailed technical reporting and AWS's own announcements.
- The FTC found contractual control, exclusivity, and cloud-spend commitments in these partnerships: confirmed by the Section 6(b) report. Specific terms for Amazon–Anthropic are redacted.
- Anthropic is pursuing multi-cloud diversification (Google TPU deal Oct 2025, Broadcom/Google 3.5 GW deal April 2026): confirmed by SEC filing and multiple credible news sources.
- Anthropic has no revenue-sharing arrangement with Amazon equivalent to OpenAI's 20% of all revenue to Microsoft: consistent across multiple analyst sources, though not confirmed by a primary source (no publicly available contract language).

**Conflicts and tensions between sources**
- The FTC report and the official Amazon/Anthropic statements are in tension. The FTC found "consultation, control, and exclusivity rights" and cloud-spend commitments; the official statements say Amazon has no decision-making power and Anthropic is free to work with any provider. Both can be true simultaneously — structural leverage and contractual access rights do not necessarily require a board seat.
- Analyst commentary differs on whether the Google TPU deal signals Amazon losing primacy (Jefferies analyst Blayne Curtis, UBS analyst Karl Keirstead) vs. a neutral diversification play. AWS formally pushed back, telling SiliconANGLE in October 2025: "The strength of our strategic collaboration with Anthropic remains unchanged, and AWS continues to be both Anthropic's primary training partner and cloud provider."
- The Mrinank Sharma departure is reported through low-credibility channels (Indian TV news social media posts) with language about "pressures to set aside what matters most" — which could refer to competitive pressure on safety timelines industry-wide rather than specific Amazon influence. This is unverified and potentially mischaracterized.

**Gaps and under-explored areas**
1. The exact contractual terms of the Amazon–Anthropic investment agreement — specifically what "consultation, control, and exclusivity rights" Amazon holds — are redacted in the FTC report and not publicly disclosed. This is the single largest gap.
2. What percentage of Anthropic's revenue flows through Amazon Bedrock specifically, vs. direct API sales, vs. other cloud partners? The CNBC Oct 2025 piece says "While Google is powering Anthropic's next phase of compute expansion, Amazon remains its most deeply embedded partner" and AWS is "considered Anthropic's chief cloud provider, making its influence structural and not just financial" — but no specific revenue percentage is cited.
3. The LTBT's own governance: who appoints LTBT members? If the founders effectively control LTBT membership, it is an independence structure. If investors have any say in LTBT composition, the shield is weaker. Not found in available sources.
4. The cloud-spend commitment: the FTC notes AI developers must spend "a large portion of their CSP partner's investment on cloud services from their partner." For Anthropic, this would mean a large fraction of the $8B Amazon investment must be spent back on AWS. This is not publicly quantified.
5. The Sharma resignation: the specific language about "pressures to set aside what matters most" is potentially highly significant but is sourced only through secondhand social media reports. Primary verification needed.

**Surprises**
- The bilateral dependency is underappreciated: Anthropic contributed 1–2pp to AWS's quarterly growth and is projected to contribute 5+pp in H2 2025. Amazon is not purely extracting from Anthropic — it needs Anthropic's success to validate its chip strategy (Trainium's commercial viability depends substantially on Anthropic using it at scale).
- Anthropic's GAAP revenue recognition structure is structurally more favorable than OpenAI's: Anthropic is the principal in cloud resales and keeps 100% of revenue (paying cloud commissions as a cost), whereas OpenAI gives Microsoft 20% off the top. This means Anthropic's financial relationship with Amazon is less extractive in revenue terms than the OpenAI–Microsoft relationship, even though Amazon has contributed more raw capital.
- The April 2026 Broadcom/Google 3.5 GW deal is massive — at $21B in chip orders alone, it dwarfs the total Amazon investment. By compute capacity, Anthropic is on track to be more Google-compute-dependent (4.5 GW via Google TPUs by 2027) than AWS-dependent (Project Rainier, size not disclosed in GW terms). The "Amazon capture" hypothesis was far more plausible in 2024 than it is in mid-2026.
- Amazon's effective equity stake at a $380B valuation is tiny (~2.1%) despite an $8B investment. This suggests the investment is structured primarily as a commercial partnership (cloud-spend commitments + chip validation) rather than a traditional equity stake seeking capital appreciation. This reframes the relationship: Amazon is less a financial investor seeking ownership and more a customer/partner buying strategic positioning in the frontier model layer.

## The Sharpest Question This Angle Offers

**Falsifiable question**: Do the cloud-spend commitments in Amazon's investment agreements with Anthropic — specifically, the requirement that Anthropic spend a specified portion of the $8B investment on AWS cloud services — constitute functional exclusivity for foundation model training, and has this contractual commitment materially constrained Anthropic's ability to run primary training runs on Google TPU or Nvidia/non-AWS infrastructure between 2024 and 2026?

This question is falsifiable because: (a) the FTC obtained the contracts under Section 6(b) and found cloud-spend commitments exist, but redacted the amounts; (b) Anthropic's own blog post says it will use Trainium "to train our most advanced foundation models," while simultaneously signing a 1GW Google TPU deal and a 3.5GW Broadcom deal; (c) if the Google/Broadcom deals are predominantly for inference rather than pre-training of flagship models, that would be consistent with AWS capturing primary training but not secondary workloads — and would represent a meaningful form of lock-in even in a nominally multi-cloud architecture; (d) if Anthropic's next frontier model (Claude 5 or equivalent) is trained primarily on AWS Trainium, that is strong evidence of de facto training exclusivity; if it is trained on Google TPUs or mixed infrastructure, that falsifies the capture claim on the most important dimension.

The question matters because compute for pre-training of frontier models is qualitatively different from inference infrastructure — pre-training requires deep hardware-software co-optimization, creates long-term chip lock-in, and is where safety research architectures are embedded at the hardware level. If Amazon controls the training substrate for frontier Claude models, it has structural influence over Anthropic's safety research capacity in a way that no formal governance document records.
