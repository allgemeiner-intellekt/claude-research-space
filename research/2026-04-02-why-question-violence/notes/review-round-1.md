# Review -- Round 1

## Overall Assessment

The four surveys have produced strong raw material across all four questions. The formal presupposition survey (Q1) is excellent -- deep, well-sourced, and already synthesized. The Aristotle/methodology survey (Q2) is solid on the philosophical lineage but thinner on the bridge to modern social science. The cases survey (Q3) is rich with examples but reads as a catalogue rather than an argument. The pragmatics survey (Q4) is broad and genuinely surprising in places (the German warum/wieso finding, the MI prohibition). However, the four threads remain largely disconnected. The essay's central argument -- that the formal presuppositional structure of why-questions is the *mechanism* by which epistemic violence operates, running from Aristotle through philosophy of science to real social harm -- has not yet been assembled. The pieces are here; the connective tissue is not.

Three specific weaknesses need addressing before report-writing:

1. **The central mechanism is under-articulated.** Van Fraassen's third presupposition (that an explanation of the right type exists) is identified as distinctive to why-questions, and Teo's epistemological violence is identified as the harm -- but no survey traces the precise causal chain: why-question posed --> presupposition accommodated unconsciously (Lewis) --> explanandum treated as established --> causal inquiry proceeds on false ground --> interpretive violence follows. This chain needs to be made explicit and tested against the case material.

2. **The Aristotle-to-social-science bridge is gestured at but not built.** The Gelman-Imbens paper is flagged as "essentially Aristotelian without knowing it," which is a genuinely interesting claim -- but it needs to be developed with precision. What exactly does the forward/reverse causal distinction share with hoti/dioti, and where does the analogy break down?

3. **The contrast class as a vehicle for ideology is the essay's most original potential contribution, but it remains a bullet point.** Van Fraassen's contrast class is identified in Q1 as under-explored in its rhetorical consequences; Q3's cases implicitly involve contrast-class manipulation (Block on "why do races differ?" rather than "why does this measurement vary?"); Q4 identifies the "taxonomic why-question" as under-theorized. These three threads converge on the same insight but no survey develops it.

## Per-Question Evaluation

### Q1: Formal structure of presupposition in why-questions
- **Coverage**: Strong. Van Fraassen's tripartite structure, Bromberger's p-predicament, Tomioka's grammatical stickiness, Macagno's burden-of-proof reversal, and the QUD framework are all well-covered with primary or authoritative secondary sources.
- **Strengths**: The identification of van Fraassen's *third* presupposition (existence of an explanation) as unique to why-questions is the essay's formal backbone. Tomioka's intervention-effects evidence providing *grammatical* support for presuppositional stickiness is a genuinely surprising finding. The Macagno burden-reversal mechanism is well-described.
- **Gaps**: (a) The survey mentions Adler (2008) on attention-direction but did not access the full text -- his argument that why-questions direct attention *away from* questioning the framework is precisely the cognitive mechanism the essay needs. (b) The QUD framework's implication -- that the existence-of-explanation presupposition is *structurally off-topic* in any why-question-governed discourse -- is stated but not developed. This is a powerful formal result that deserves deeper treatment. (c) No engagement with whether the presuppositional structure of why-questions differs cross-linguistically (the Egbert/Voge warum/wieso finding from Q4 is relevant here but the two surveys don't talk to each other).

### Q2: Aristotelian hoti/dioti distinction
- **Coverage**: Good on Aristotle's primary text and the Hempel parallel. Weaker on the bridge to contemporary methodology.
- **Strengths**: The direct Aristotle quotations from Posterior Analytics II.1 are clean and usable. The Gruender paper making the Aristotle-Hempel parallel explicit is a valuable find. The Gelman-Imbens connection is the survey's best insight.
- **Gaps**: (a) The survey flags that no philosopher has explicitly argued that premature why-questioning is a *systematic methodological error* -- this is identified as a potential original contribution but not developed. (b) The continental tradition (Gadamer's hermeneutic circle, the Verstehen/Erklaren debate) is mentioned as likely relevant but completely unexplored. This matters because Teo (Q3/Q4) explicitly draws on Gadamer. (c) The connection between van Fraassen's first presupposition (topic must be true) and Aristotle's ordering principle (establish hoti before seeking dioti) is noted as a formal encoding but not traced carefully -- does van Fraassen cite Aristotle? Does anyone make this connection explicitly?

### Q3: Concrete cases of premature why-asking
- **Coverage**: Rich case material across race/IQ (Block), gender/neuroscience (Fine, Fausto-Sterling), ego depletion, stereotype threat, and culture of poverty. Teo provides the theoretical frame.
- **Strengths**: Block (1996) is excellent -- the corn-seed example and the indirect-heritability argument are philosophically precise demonstrations of the premature-why mechanism. The ego depletion case is well-documented with the Hagger et al. replication failure. The culture-of-poverty case is a strong policy example.
- **Gaps**: (a) The cases are presented as a list, not as instances of a single mechanism. The essay needs them unified under the formal apparatus from Q1. Specifically: for each case, what exactly was the presupposition smuggled in by the why-question? What was the contrast class? What would a properly ordered inquiry (hoti-first) have looked like? (b) No case from the natural sciences. The survey focuses entirely on social science, which risks making this look like a critique specific to politically charged research. A natural-science case (e.g., "why do ulcers recur?" before establishing that they are caused by H. pylori rather than stress; or phlogiston-era chemistry asking "why does phlogiston escape during combustion?") would strengthen the argument that the mechanism is general. (c) The ego depletion and stereotype threat cases are slightly different from the others -- they involve premature mechanism-seeking for an unstable *effect*, not premature causal attribution to a *group*. The essay should distinguish these two failure modes rather than conflating them.

### Q4: Pragmatics and social force of why-questions
- **Coverage**: Broad and genuinely cross-disciplinary. CA material (Bolden & Robinson, Koshik, Egbert & Voge) is the strongest thread. Levinas is gestured at but not developed.
- **Strengths**: Bolden & Robinson's "Janus-faced" characterization is precise and usable. The warum/wieso finding from Egbert & Voge is an unexpected gem. The MI prohibition is a striking piece of applied evidence. Fricker's epistemic injustice framework is correctly identified as a productive but unmade connection.
- **Gaps**: (a) The major gap identified by the survey itself -- no literature connects presuppositional structure (Q1) with social function (Q4) -- is real and is the essay's central argumentative challenge. This gap cannot be filled by finding more sources; it requires the essay to build the bridge itself. But it would help to have deeper engagement with Macagno (Q1) on burden-of-proof reversal as the specific mechanism linking formal structure to social power. (b) The Levinas thread is too thin. The Altez (2007) secondary source is not enough to ground a serious claim about phenomenological violence. Either this thread should be developed with primary Levinas text (Totality and Infinity on the face, the relation between comprehension and possession) or it should be dropped. (c) The survey notes that "who is permitted to ask why is socially structured" (the hierarchy finding) but does not connect this to the research-methodology cases in Q3. In research, the scientist asking "why does group X have property Y?" occupies a structural position of epistemic authority analogous to the superior using warum in a business meeting. This connection is important and missing.

## Deep-Dive Assignments

### Assignment 1: The contrast class as ideological instrument
- **Question to answer**: How does the selection of a contrast class in a why-question function as an ideological act? Trace this through van Fraassen's formal apparatus and then through at least two of the case studies (Block on race/IQ, and the culture-of-poverty framing). For each case, identify: (a) the implicit contrast class in the why-question as actually posed, (b) what alternative contrast classes would have been available, (c) how the choice of contrast class pre-determined which explanations were visible.
- **Why it matters**: This is the essay's most original potential contribution. Van Fraassen's contrast class is a formal concept; the cases show it operating as a mechanism of ideological selection. No one in the literature has connected these explicitly. If done well, this becomes the essay's central argument: that the contrast class is where the violence lives -- not in the question's surface form, but in its hidden presuppositional structure.
- **Where to start**: Van Fraassen (1980) ch. 5 on contrast classes; Lipton (1990) on contrastive explanation; Block (1996) on framing the race-IQ question; Harvey & Reed (1996) on the culture-of-poverty framing. Also check whether Longino (1990) discusses contrast classes or question-framing explicitly.

### Assignment 2: The accommodation mechanism -- from Lewis to epistemic violence
- **Question to answer**: Trace the precise chain from Lewis's (1979) presupposition accommodation through to Teo's epistemological violence. The claim to develop: when a why-question is posed in a research context, its presuppositions are accommodated into the common ground of the discipline -- not just the immediate conversational context but the research program's background assumptions. Once accommodated, the presupposition (that the phenomenon exists, that it has an explanation of a certain type) becomes invisible infrastructure. How does this accommodation work in the specific case of a research question that frames an entire field? Does the QUD framework (Beaver et al. 2017) help formalize how a why-question structures an entire discourse so that its presuppositions are permanently off-topic?
- **Why it matters**: The essay's argument depends on showing that accommodation is not a one-shot conversational event but a field-structuring process. If a why-question becomes the governing Question Under Discussion for a research program, then its presuppositions shape decades of inquiry. This is the mechanism that connects the philosophy of language to the philosophy of science and to the real-world cases.
- **Where to start**: Lewis (1979) "Scorekeeping in a Language Game"; Stalnaker (1974, 2002) on common ground; Beaver et al. (2017) QUD framework; Teo (2008, 2010); Longino (1990) on background assumptions. The key question is whether anyone has applied accommodation theory to the level of research-program framing rather than single-conversation pragmatics.

### Assignment 3: A natural-science case of premature why-questioning
- **Question to answer**: Find and develop at least one case from natural science (not social science) where asking "why" before establishing "that" led to a distorted research program. The strongest candidates: (a) the stress-ulcer hypothesis -- decades of research asking "why does stress cause ulcers?" before Marshall and Warren established that H. pylori was the actual cause, meaning the presupposition ("stress causes ulcers") was false; (b) phlogiston theory asking "why does phlogiston escape during combustion?" when the presupposed substance did not exist; (c) aether theory asking "why does light propagate through the aether?" For the chosen case, trace the why-question's presuppositional structure using van Fraassen's framework: what was the topic, the contrast class, and the relevance relation? When and how was the presupposition finally challenged?
- **Why it matters**: The current case material is entirely from social science, which makes the argument look like it is about politics rather than epistemology. A natural-science case demonstrates that the mechanism is general: premature why-questioning distorts inquiry regardless of whether the topic is politically charged. This also connects back to Aristotle, whose examples (twinkling planets, moon phases) are from natural philosophy.
- **Where to start**: For the ulcer case: Barry Marshall's Nobel lecture (2005); Thagard (1998) "Ulcers and Bacteria" in studies of conceptual change. For phlogiston: Kuhn's *Structure of Scientific Revolutions* on paradigm shifts; Chang (2012) *Is Water H2O?* For aether: Swenson (1972) *The Ethereal Aether*.

## Verdict

**CONTINUE** -- with the three deep-dive assignments above.

The material is strong but the argument is not yet assembled. The three assignments target the three connective joints the essay needs: (1) the contrast class as the specific site where formal structure meets ideological content, (2) the accommodation mechanism as the process that scales presupposition from conversation to research program, and (3) a natural-science case that proves the generality of the mechanism. Without these, the report would be a competent survey of four related literatures. With them, it can be a genuine argument.
