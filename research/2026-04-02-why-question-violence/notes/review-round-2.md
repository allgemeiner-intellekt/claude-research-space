## Remaining Minor Issues (Not Requiring Further Investigation)

1. **The essay should distinguish two failure modes**: (a) premature why-questioning about groups (Block, poverty, Fine) where the violence is in naturalizing a contested social category as the unit of contrast, and (b) premature why-questioning about mechanisms (ego depletion, H. pylori) where the violence is in presupposing a causal structure that forecloses the correct answer. Both are contrast class errors but they operate differently. The orchestrator should make this distinction rather than conflating them.

2. **The scaling argument needs explicit treatment.** The move from conversational accommodation to disciplinary accommodation is the chain's weakest joint. The orchestrator should dedicate a paragraph to it, citing Stalnaker's own caveat about anonymous audiences, and then giving concrete examples of how disciplinary common ground is maintained (grant application templates, textbook introductions, review article framings, editorial scope statements). This makes the analogical extension concrete rather than hand-waving.

3. **The essay should be clear about what it is not arguing.** It is not arguing that all why-questions are violent, that why-questions should never be asked, or that causal inquiry is inherently harmful. The argument is structural and specific: why-questions carry presuppositions that can be weaponized through contrast class selection, and the accommodation mechanism renders this weaponization invisible. The "5 whys" in lean manufacturing, Socratic questioning, and productive scientific why-questions all exist. The violence is in a specific configuration, not in the question form itself.
